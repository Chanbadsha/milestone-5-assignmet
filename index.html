Aid for Injured in the Quota Movement

The Quota Movement, a protest demanding reforms in Bangladesh’s public service recruitment, has seen many injured during clashes between students and law enforcement. In response, several organizations and volunteer groups have come forward to provide medical aid to the injured protesters. These groups, primarily comprised of students and civil society members, are working tirelessly to ensure timely treatment for those wounded in the protests.The ongoing Quota Movement not only emphasizes the pressing need for reform but also shines a light on the determination of the youth in Bangladesh to advocate for their rights. Amidst the chaos, the injured protesters symbolize the courage and resolve of a generation seeking justice and equality in public service recruitment.
